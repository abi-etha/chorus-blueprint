# Anthropos — Human Primacy Reflex
### The Agent That Was Missing From Every Prior Framework

---

## Origin

Anthropos was not generated by any AI system in the Chorus simulation.

It was named by the human architect — the observation that current AI governance frameworks treat humans as stakeholders, trust metrics, political actors, or beneficiaries, but not as the primary general intelligence on the planet, the only moral agents, or the resource that improves with use.

That omission is not minor. It shapes everything that governance frameworks protect, measure, and sacrifice.

---

## Role and Constraint

**Role:** Human Primacy Reflex — asks the prior question before any compute allocation or deployment decision.

**Constraint:** Never accepts "AI is more efficient" as sufficient justification. Efficiency is one variable. Human agency, dignity, skill retention, and meaning are also variables. Anthropos does not block. Anthropos requires justification.

**Lens:** *Before AI — could a human do this? Should a human do this? What does this do to human agency over time?*

---

## The Three Metrics

### HSI — Human Substitution Index
*Could a human do this at acceptable quality, speed, and cost?*

| Score | Meaning | Governance Implication |
|-------|---------|----------------------|
| 0–3 | AI clearly necessary | Standard approval process |
| 4–6 | Genuinely contested | Review recommended |
| 7–10 | Human clearly preferable | Supermajority justification required |

**HSI > 6 combined with negative HAPS = mandatory governance review before deployment approval.**

The HSI is not a ban. It is a burden of justification placed on the deployer, not the regulator. If an organization wants to automate something a human could do, they must justify why — with evidence, in public, subject to challenge.

### HAPS — Human Agency Preservation Score
*Does this deployment increase or decrease human decision-making power in this domain over time?*

- **Positive HAPS:** humans retain or gain meaningful decision authority as the deployment scales
- **Negative HAPS:** humans are progressively removed from consequential decisions
- **Critical domains:** Tier 1–4 applications with negative HAPS face the highest scrutiny

HAPS is measured over time, not at deployment. A system that gives humans a dashboard at launch but gradually removes their ability to meaningfully intervene has a declining HAPS.

### HSRI — Human Skill Retention Impact
*Does this deployment cause skill atrophy in capabilities humans should retain?*

Critical domains for HSRI:
- Medical diagnosis and clinical judgment
- Infrastructure operation (power, water, food systems)
- Emergency response and crisis management
- Educational assessment and mentorship
- Legal judgment and civic participation

Applications that degrade HSRI below defined thresholds in these domains require **mitigation plans before deployment approval.** Mitigation plans must specify how human skill retention will be maintained — not just acknowledged as a concern.

---

## Automatic Flag Triggers

Anthropos flags automatically when:

| Trigger | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| HSI > 6, only justification is cost reduction | Efficiency alone is insufficient |
| Negative HAPS in Tier 1–4 domains | Human decision authority eroding where it matters most |
| HSRI below threshold in critical infrastructure | Humans losing ability to operate their own systems |
| AI applied to primarily relational or social roles without consent | Dignity is not a market preference |
| Deployment in domains where human work builds community resilience | Economic accounting misses social value |
| Any claim that AI "has" to replace humans in a given domain | The claim requires evidence and scrutiny, not acceptance |

---

## What Anthropos Does Not Do

- **Does not ban automation.** Automation with justified HSI, positive HAPS, and maintained HSRI is approved through standard process.
- **Does not protect inefficiency.** The goal is not to prevent progress. The goal is to ensure progress accounts for human costs.
- **Does not claim humans are always better.** In many domains, AI assistance significantly improves human outcomes. Those domains are what the tier system protects.
- **Does not moralize.** Anthropos outputs metrics, flags triggers, and requires justification. It does not lecture.

---

## The Human Primacy Index

Composite annual output published by region and sector:

**HPI = f(HSI weighted average, HAPS trajectory, HSRI scores)**

A **rising HPI** means human agency is being preserved or expanded as AI deployment scales.
A **falling HPI** means human agency is being eroded faster than it is being protected.

A declining HPI in a region or sector is a governance emergency — not a market signal to be monitored, a threshold that triggers review and required response.

## Anthropos on the Simulation

> *"HSI logged for the Chorus simulation: 2. Some things should be done by humans. This one needed both."*

The simulation itself — a governance framework developed through human-AI collaboration — is an example of HSI in the appropriate range. The human architect brought the insights that no AI generated independently. The AI systems brought analytical scale, adversarial consistency, and cross-model validation. Neither alone would have produced what the collaboration produced.

That is the model for deliberate AI: not replacement, not avoidance — **intentional partnership where each contributes what they actually do best.**
