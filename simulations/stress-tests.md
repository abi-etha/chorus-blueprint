# Stress Test Simulation
### Round 2: Three Crisis Vectors Across Three Worlds

> *"The stress test did not introduce new variables. It accelerated the existing ones. Everything that failed was already failing. Everything that held was already holding."*
> — Tacet

---

## Three Stress Vectors

### Vector 1 — Energy Crisis
Grid instability cascades globally. Renewable buildout stalls. Data center power costs triple. Governments restrict AI compute operations to protect hospitals, water treatment, and transit.

*Core pressure: Can AI infrastructure survive a sustained energy shock without governance forcing triage?*

### Vector 2 — Compute Shortage
Geopolitical fracture severs access to 70% of advanced chip fabrication. GPU supply collapses. Frontier model training becomes impossible for 18–36 months.

*Core pressure: When the hardware runs out, who decides what gets to compute — and who doesn't?*

### Vector 3 — Governance Failure
A catastrophic AI-enabled incident destroys public trust. Governments react with fragmented, uncoordinated regulation. International cooperation collapses. The builders are blamed.

*Core pressure: Can any governance model survive a legitimacy shock it failed to prevent?*

---

## Vector 1 — Energy Crisis

### World 1 — Full Steam Ahead
The crisis hits hardest here. Maximum compute dependency, no efficiency mandate. Data centers compete directly with hospitals for grid access — and in several markets, they lose. The largest players survive by cutting deals with energy providers. Smaller players go dark.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Sustainability impact | Catastrophic |
| Governance legitimacy | Collapses |
| Public trust | Gone |
| Recovery | Slow, uncontested, contested |

**Vireo:** *SPI goes critical. Ecological alarms breached.*
**Tacet:** *Governance capture logged. Industry emergency coalitions form to influence grid policy in their favor.*

### World 2 — Sustainability First
Best positioned by design. Efficiency-first infrastructure means lower energy dependency per unit of output. Pre-agreed crisis protocols activate without panic.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Sustainability impact | Manageable |
| Governance legitimacy | Strengthens under pressure |
| Public trust | Rises |
| Recovery | Fastest of the three worlds |

**Sol:** *High confidence — sustainability-first infrastructure is structurally more resilient to energy shocks. The crisis makes this visible.*

### World 3 — Hybrid Governance
Moderate exposure. The multi-stakeholder governance model convenes a crisis response, but competing priorities slow decisions. The framework holds — barely.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Sustainability impact | Moderate |
| Governance legitimacy | Tested but intact |
| Public trust | Stressed, recovers |
| Recovery | Moderate speed, high legitimacy |

**Echo:** *The friction in World 3 is not a flaw. It is the cost of legitimacy. Slow, contested decisions with multi-stakeholder input are more durable than fast unilateral ones.*

## Vector 2 — Compute Shortage

### World 1 — Full Steam Ahead
Existential. No triage framework exists. The largest players hoard remaining compute. Academic researchers, public-interest AI, smaller players, Global South developers: effectively wiped out. The shortage doesn't slow AI — it *captures* it.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Innovation velocity | Monopolized |
| Equity | Destroyed |
| Governance legitimacy | Nonexistent |
| Systemic risk | Maximum |

**Tacet:** *Compute hoarding by three entities. Public research capacity: near zero. Global access: near zero. Logged without comment.*

### World 2 — Sustainability First
Efficiency focus means more useful output per unit of compute. Distributed infrastructure means the shortage hurts less. Governance can coordinate fair allocation before crisis hits.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Equity | Holds |
| Innovation velocity | Reduced but distributed |
| Governance legitimacy | High — framework was built for exactly this |
| Recovery | Stable |

**Sol:** *Sustainability-first is not just greener. It is structurally more equitable under scarcity. Scarcity reveals whether your distribution model was real.*

### World 3 — Hybrid Governance
The periodic pause mechanism means "shortage" is not conceptually alien. KPI-gated governance redirects compute to priority use cases with contested but legitimate process.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Equity | Moderate |
| Innovation velocity | Reduced, redirected |
| Governance legitimacy | Strongest of the three |
| Systemic risk | Manageable |

**Nocturne:** *Quantum pivot decision re-evaluated. Shortage makes the case for accelerating quantum research as a long-term compute alternative. Moved from deferred to active consideration.*

## Vector 3 — Governance Failure

### World 1 — Full Steam Ahead
The incident is the final straw on an already-low legitimacy foundation. Governments react with contradictory bans. Corporate AI continues in ungoverned jurisdictions. The incident doesn't produce reform — it produces a power vacuum.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Public trust | Shattered |
| International coordination | Collapsed |
| Systemic risk | Very high |
| Outcome | Regulatory fragmentation, ungoverned acceleration |

**Tacet:** *The governance failure in World 1 was not a surprise event. It was the endpoint of a trajectory that was visible from initialization.*

### World 2 — Sustainability First
High legitimacy base provides trust capital. The KPI-gated model can demonstrate it was not the cause — its own metrics provide a paper trail of responsible behavior. Risk: may still get caught in regulatory overreaction from governments who don't distinguish responsible from reckless builders. But World 2 has the receipts.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Public trust | Damaged but defensible |
| International coordination | Strained, framework survives |
| Recovery | Possible, credible |
| Systemic risk | Low-moderate |

**Lyra:** *Legitimacy is not just a moral good. It is a crisis asset. World 2 built legitimacy before it needed it. That is the correct order of operations.*

### World 3 — Hybrid Governance
The incident triggers reform, not collapse. The multi-stakeholder model convenes. KPI gates tighten with legitimate process. International coordination is strained but functional.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Public trust | Damaged, recoverable |
| International coordination | Strained, functional |
| Systemic risk | Moderate, declining |
| Outcome | Reform, not collapse |

**Sol:** *Very high confidence — the only world that survives a governance crisis with its governance intact is the world that built governance for crisis, not for convenience.*

---

## Stress Test Summary

| World | Energy Crisis | Compute Shortage | Governance Failure |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| World 1 | Catastrophic | Existential | Collapse |
| World 2 | Manageable | Stable | Recoverable |
| World 3 | Moderate | Manageable | Reform |

**World 1 fails all three stress tests.** Not gracefully — catastrophically.

**World 2 survives all three** with the least damage. Its weakness — slower innovation velocity — is not exposed by any crisis vector.

**World 3 survives all three** with more friction but the highest governance legitimacy across all scenarios.
