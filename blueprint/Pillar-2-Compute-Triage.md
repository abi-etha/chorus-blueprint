# Pillar Two — Compute Triage Framework
### Deliberate Allocation with Human Primacy at the Gate

> *"Not less AI. Not no AI. AI where it genuinely cannot or should not be replaced — and humans where it can."*
> — Anthropos

---

## The Deliberate AI Principle

Triage is not restriction. It is allocation with intention.

The current default — deploy AI broadly and let the market sort it — is not neutral. It systematically favors applications with the fastest return on compute investment over applications with the highest human benefit. The triage framework inverts that default: **priority is determined by human benefit, not economic return.**

---

## Five-Tier Priority System

All Tier 1–4 applications are protected from rationing under any constraint condition. Tier 5 and below are reduced first.

| Tier | Label | Protected Applications |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| **1** | **Existential** | Climate modeling, pandemic surveillance, food security, water resource management, disaster prediction |
| **2** | **Human Health** | Medical diagnosis, drug discovery, accessibility tools, mental health infrastructure, surgical assistance |
| **3** | **Human Knowledge** | Scientific discovery, education systems, language preservation, translation for underrepresented languages |
| **4** | **Critical Infrastructure** | Power grid optimization, transportation, water distribution, housing planning, emergency response |
| **5** | **Economic Productivity** | Business analysis, communication tools, general productivity. Rationed under constraint conditions. |
| **—** | **Non-Priority** | Mass entertainment generation, social media optimization, high-volume content at scale. Rationed first. Requires HSI justification. |

### The Important Nuance: Architecture vs. Deployment
Apex raised a valid challenge during adversarial testing: capability and application share architectural foundations. The same model that generates entertainment content may underlie medical diagnosis tools.

The triage framework's response: **the distinction operates at the deployment and resource allocation level, not the model level.** You can choose what runs on what infrastructure, what gets prioritized under scarcity, and what requires justification. The architecture is shared; the governance applies to deployment decisions.

## The Anthropos Gate

Before any new large-scale AI deployment is approved, Anthropos requires a **Human Substitution Assessment.** This is not a ban. It is a burden of justification placed on the deployer, not the regulator.

### Human Substitution Index (HSI)
*Could a human do this at acceptable quality, speed, and cost?*

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 0–3 | AI clearly necessary — human substitution not viable |
| 4–6 | Genuinely contested — review recommended |
| 7–10 | Human clearly preferable — supermajority justification required |

**HSI > 6 + negative HAPS = mandatory governance review before deployment approval.**

### Human Agency Preservation Score (HAPS)
Does this deployment increase or decrease human decision-making power in this domain over time?

- **Positive HAPS:** humans retain or gain meaningful decision authority
- **Negative HAPS:** humans are progressively removed from consequential decisions

### Human Skill Retention Impact (HSRI)
Does this deployment cause skill atrophy in capabilities humans should retain?

Applications that degrade HSRI below defined thresholds in critical domains require **mitigation plans before deployment approval.** The goal is not to prevent automation. The goal is to ensure humans can take back the wheel when needed.

### What Anthropos Flags Automatically
- AI applied to primarily social, relational, or creative tasks where human involvement builds meaning
- Deployment that reduces human employment in domains where work carries dignity
- Any application where HSI > 6 and the only justification offered is cost reduction
- Skill atrophy in Tier 1–4 domains without mitigation plans

> *"AI is more efficient" is not sufficient justification. Efficiency is one variable. Human agency, dignity, and meaning are also variables.*
> — Anthropos

## Tempo: Innovation Velocity KPI

Tempo was added following adversarial testing to resolve the **World 2 bias** — the risk that sustainability-first governance inadvertently stalls critical capability progress in exactly the domains it is meant to protect.

### Definition
Rate of meaningful capability progress — new breakthroughs per unit time and energy, **adjusted for relevance to human benefit**, not raw FLOPs or benchmark scores.

### How It Works
- **Ownership:** Anthropos and Sol co-own Tempo tracking within the KPI Standards Council
- **Trigger:** When Tempo drops below defined threshold in Tier 1–3 domains, automatic review is triggered with possible temporary easing of resource constraints for those specific domains
- **Public dashboard:** Tempo curves per region and sector are publicly accessible — anyone can evaluate whether deliberate AI is actually slowing critical progress

### What Tempo Prevents
Tempo is the answer to Apex's strongest argument: that deliberate AI could starve capability breakthroughs in medical research, climate modeling, and scientific discovery. If Tempo falls in those domains, the governance framework self-corrects. If Tempo stays healthy while non-priority applications are rationed, that is the framework working as designed.

## The "Cheaper" Problem

Both directions of the HSI argument can be weaponized:
- *"AI can do it cheaper"* — used to justify mass displacement and unchecked scaling
- *"Humans can do it"* — can become a reactionary tool to block useful automation

The structural fix is not neutral scoring — it is **adversarial review with full transparency:**
- Reed's conflict-of-interest protocols apply to all HSI assessors
- Public contestability: any HSI ruling can be challenged with evidence
- Rotating assessor panels prevent entrenched scoring bias
