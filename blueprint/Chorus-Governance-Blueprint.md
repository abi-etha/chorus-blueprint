### Preserving Human Primacy in the Age of AI

> *Version 1.0 — June 2026*
> *Developed through four rounds of multi-AI collaborative simulation across Claude (Anthropic), Grok (xAI), Copilot (Microsoft), and one human architect.*

---

## The Central Question

The challenge of AI governance is not *"how do we control AI."*

It is **how do we preserve humanity's agency, dignity, and creative primacy** while harnessing AI where it genuinely matters.

This framework — forged through simulation, stress testing, and adversarial pressure — rejects both unchecked acceleration and naive degrowth. The answer is **deliberate AI**: AI as a scalpel, not a default replacement.

---

## Guiding Principles

- **Humans are the planet's most valuable and renewable resource.**
- AI must serve human flourishing, not replace it by default.
- Governance must be structurally resistant to capture.
- Ecological limits are hard constraints, not preferences.
- Legitimacy and resilience are the same thing measured at different timescales.

---

## The Core Tension

> *"AI is being built by the system it may need to help us replace. Therefore the system must be redesigned to allow replacement — safely, slowly, and with legitimacy."*
> — Chorus Ensemble Convergence, Round 1

The builder-as-regulator problem is real. The entities with the resources to implement this framework are precisely the entities with the most financial incentive not to. The blueprint does not pretend otherwise. It designs for that reality.

## Key Innovations

### Anthropos — Human Primacy Reflex
Requires explicit justification before AI displaces meaningful human work. Outputs:
- **HSI** — Human Substitution Index (0–10: can a human do this?)
- **HAPS** — Human Agency Preservation Score
- **HSRI** — Human Skill Retention Impact

### Reed — Capture Auditor
Permanent governance spine. Asks: who funds, who staffs, who writes the agenda, who benefits? Does not intervene. Names things.

### Tempo — Innovation Velocity KPI
Tracks meaningful capability progress per unit resource in Tier 1–3 domains. Prevents "deliberate AI" from becoming innovation austerity.

### Vireo — Ecological Layer
Maintains the Sustainability Pressure Index (SPI). The data was always there. The blueprint gives it teeth.

### Tacet — Silent Auditor
Watches for runaway feedback loops, governance capture, hidden assumptions. Logs. Never intervenes.

## Sol's Confidence Assessment

**High confidence:** Rotating seats, sunset clauses, compute levy, five-tier triage, HSI gate, Tempo as anti-stagnation mechanism.

**Medium confidence:** Federated regional structure (if regions have genuine power), international treaty framework (achievable, not guaranteed), resource metering rollout.

**Low confidence:** Implementation before a major crisis forces it; that entities with most to lose do not delay adoption; that quantum computing changes the calculus near-term.
