## International Coordination Models

**Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT)**
The treaty network model in Pillar Four draws explicitly on the NPT's structure — not as an ideal to replicate, but as evidence that international coordination on dangerous technology is achievable and has meaningful effect even without perfect compliance.

**International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA)**
The independent verification model — structurally separated from standard-setting — is directly informed by IAEA's organizational structure.

**Montreal Protocol on Substances that Deplete the Ozone Layer**
One of the most successful international environmental agreements. Instructive for the question of how to build compliance incentives into treaty frameworks.

## Political Philosophy & Systems Design

**On power and obligation:** The principle that power creates obligation — referenced in the blueprint as predating Spider-Man — appears in Burke, Voltaire, and Theodore Roosevelt, among others. The formulation "with great power comes great responsibility" as a political-ethical claim has roots in the French National Convention, 1793.

**Capture theory:** The regulatory capture framework originates with George Stigler's 1971 paper "The Theory of Economic Regulation." Reed's five-question test is informed by subsequent capture theory literature.

**Systems thinking:** Donella Meadows' *Thinking in Systems* (2008) is foundational for the Chorus simulation's state-variable approach and its attention to feedback loops, delays, and leverage points.

---

## Labor & Human Primacy

**Daron Acemoglu & Simon Johnson — Power and Progress (2023)**
Makes the economic and historical case that technological progress does not automatically benefit workers, and that the distribution of AI's benefits requires deliberate institutional choices. Directly relevant to the Meaningful Work Compact.

**Kate Raworth — Doughnut Economics (2017)**
A framework for economics that takes ecological limits as hard constraints and human dignity as a floor. Structurally compatible with the Chorus Blueprint's approach to ecological ceilings and human primacy.

**AI & labor displacement** — Ongoing research from Brookings Institution, MIT Work of the Future Task Force, and OECD on AI's employment effects.

## Human Primacy in AI Governance

*The specific framing of humans as the primary general intelligence, the only moral agents, and the resource that improves with use — rather than as stakeholders or trust metrics — does not have a direct antecedent in published AI governance literature as of the time of this writing. This gap is part of what the Chorus Blueprint addresses.*

Related but distinct work:
- **Human-centered AI (HCAI)** — HAI at Stanford, focusing on AI that enhances human capabilities
- **Value alignment research** — work on ensuring AI systems pursue human-compatible goals
- **Democratic AI** — research on participatory design and public accountability in AI systems
