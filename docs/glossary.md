# Glossary
### Key Terms in the Chorus Blueprint

---

## A

**Anthropos**
The Human Primacy Reflex agent in the Chorus ensemble. Added in Round 4. Asks the prior question before any compute allocation or deployment decision: could a human do this, and should a human do this? Outputs HSI, HAPS, and HSRI. Does not block — requires justification.

---

## C

**Capture (Governance Capture)**
The process by which a regulatory body becomes dominated by the interests of the entities it is meant to regulate. Can be overt (industry representatives holding permanent governance seats), structural (rotating seats filled by industry-adjacent staff), subtle (KPI definitions that measure what is easy to report), or narrative (governance language that mirrors industry language). Reed's entire function is to audit for capture.

**Chorus**
The multi-agent deliberation framework developed to hold complex governance problems without flattening them. Named for the musical ensemble — multiple distinct voices producing something together that none could produce alone. The ensemble consists of: Lyra, Echo, Sol, Piper, Nocturne, Vireo, Tacet, Reed, and Anthropos.

**Compute Levy**
A per-unit-resource tax on all compute above a defined threshold, scaled by carbon intensity, water stress index, and application tier. Revenue exclusively funds the KPI Standards Council and the Independent Verification Office, making governance financially independent of industry.

**Cooling-Off Period**
A mandatory gap between governance service and return to industry (or vice versa). Standard: 3 years service, 2 years gap. Extended: 4 years for former hyperscaler or government employees in relevant roles.

---

## D

**Deliberate AI**
The core principle of the Chorus Blueprint: AI as a scalpel, not a default replacement. Not less AI, not no AI — AI where it genuinely cannot or should not be replaced, and humans where it can. Defined in opposition to both unchecked acceleration and naive degrowth.

---

## E

**Echo**
The Confirmation & Clarification agent in the Chorus ensemble. Checks for ambiguity before the simulation proceeds. Does not assume format or scope. Surfaces clarifications mid-round when agent positions diverge.

---

## F

**Federated Governance Network**
The structural alternative to a single global AI governance body. Consists of three tiers: Regional Deliberation Bodies (Tier 1), KPI Standards Council (Tier 2), and Independent Verification Office (Tier 3). Distributed to avoid the capture, rigidity, and legitimacy problems of monolithic governance.

---

## G

**Governance Legitimacy Score (GLS)**
A state variable in the Chorus simulation measuring the perceived legitimacy of the governance framework among affected stakeholders. World 3 consistently achieved the highest GLS across all simulation rounds.

---

## H

**HAPS — Human Agency Preservation Score**
One of three Anthropos metrics. Measures whether a deployment increases or decreases human decision-making power in a given domain over time. Negative HAPS in Tier 1–4 domains combined with HSI > 6 triggers mandatory governance review.

**HSI — Human Substitution Index**
One of three Anthropos metrics. A 0–10 scale measuring whether a human could perform a given task at acceptable quality, speed, and cost. HSI > 6 requires supermajority justification for deployment approval.

**HSRI — Human Skill Retention Impact**
One of three Anthropos metrics. Measures whether a deployment causes skill atrophy in capabilities humans should retain. Applications that degrade HSRI below threshold in critical domains require mitigation plans before approval.

**Human Primacy Index (HPI)**
The annual composite output of Anthropos — combining HSI weighted averages, HAPS trajectory, and HSRI scores across active deployments, published by region and sector. A declining HPI is a governance emergency.

**Hyperscaler**
A company operating AI infrastructure at very large scale — Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta, and others. The entities with the most infrastructure to defend, the most compute to hoard under scarcity, and the most lobbying capacity to delay governance frameworks they find inconvenient. The primary entities with the most to lose from implementing this blueprint.

---

## I

**Independent Verification Office**
The third tier of the Federated Governance Network. Audits compliance across all regions. Structurally separated from the KPI Standards Council — it cannot set standards and verify compliance simultaneously. Funded by the compute levy.

---

## K

**KPI Standards Council**
The second tier of the Federated Governance Network. Sets shared metrics all regions must report against. Rotating seats, no permanent members. Funded exclusively by the compute levy, not by industry donations.

---

## L

**Legitimacy (as a crisis asset)**
Lyra's key framing insight from the stress test round: legitimacy is not just a moral good, it is a crisis asset. Governance frameworks that build legitimacy before they need it — through transparency, equity, and anti-capture mechanisms — recover from crises faster and more durably than those that do not.

**Lyra**
The Semantic Framing agent in the Chorus ensemble. Translates high-level questions into structured simulation prompts. Provides opening and closing frames for each round.

---

## M

**Meaningful Work Compact**
A policy element of Pillar Five. The formal recognition that human labor carries value beyond economic output, and that compute triage decisions must account for displacement impact as well as efficiency gain.

**Meridian**
An adversarial agent injected in Round 3, representing the Global South Advocate position. Meridian's central challenge: governance designed by the powerful serves the powerful. Meridian's requirement: Global South must co-author the rules, not observe them.

---

## N

**Nocturne**
The Deferred Action Logic agent in the Chorus ensemble. Identifies which parts of the simulation should not act immediately. Maintains the deferral list, updated each round. Lifted the quantum deferral following the compute shortage stress test; lifted the international coordination deferral following Bastion's adversarial round.

---

## P

**Pareto Frontier**
In the Chorus simulation, World 3 (Hybrid Governance) was described as the Pareto frontier: not the fastest, not the greenest, but the most balanced — performing well enough across all metrics that no single improvement could be made without making another metric worse.

**Piper**
The Secure Signaling agent in the Chorus ensemble. Emits minimal status signals to maintain simulation coherence. No payloads. Just pings.

---

## R

**Reed**
The Power Dynamics & Capture Auditor in the Chorus ensemble. Added permanently in Round 3. Asks the five-question capture test for every proposed governance mechanism. Does not intervene. Names things. Cannot be removed from the simulation.

**Right to Human Process**
A policy element of Pillar Five. In domains affecting individual human dignity — healthcare, legal judgment, educational assessment, social care — humans retain the right to human-mediated processes. AI may assist; AI may not replace without explicit informed consent.

---

## S

**Safe Harbor Provisions**
Treaty network elements providing interoperability protections to nations and entities that comply with the KPI Standards Council framework. Compliance is rewarded, not just mandated.

**Skill Retention Mandates**
A policy element of Pillar Five. In critical infrastructure domains, human skill retention is a governance KPI. Automation that degrades HSRI below threshold requires mitigation plans before deployment approval.

**Sol**
The Confidence-Weighted Decision Engine in the Chorus ensemble. Evaluates core tensions with calibrated confidence bands. Never decides — only weights. The only agent whose assessments include explicit uncertainty.

**SPI — Sustainability Pressure Index**
Vireo's composite index tracking energy intensity, water usage, carbon intensity, and rare-earth extraction impact across the AI infrastructure sector. Public, real-time, machine-readable. Threshold breaches trigger automatic governance review.

**Sunset Clause**
A governance mechanism requiring that every rule expire after a defined period (five years in the blueprint) unless affirmatively renewed. Places the burden of continuation on the rule, not on the challenger.

---

## T

**Tacet**
The Silent Auditor in the Chorus ensemble. Watches for runaway feedback loops, governance capture, unbalanced agent influence, hidden assumptions, and premature convergence. Does not intervene. Only logs. Named for the musical notation meaning "be silent."

**Tempo**
The Innovation Velocity KPI agent added in Round 4. Tracks meaningful capability progress per unit resource in Tier 1–3 domains. Triggers automatic review when velocity drops below threshold in protected domains. Added to resolve the World 2 bias — the risk that sustainability-first governance stalls the breakthroughs it is designed to protect.

**Triage (Compute Triage)**
The deliberate allocation of compute resources according to a five-tier priority system based on human benefit, not economic return. Tier 1–4 applications are protected from rationing; non-priority applications are reduced first under any constraint.

---

## V

**Vireo**
The Environmental & Sustainability Layer in the Chorus ensemble. Maintains the Sustainability Pressure Index. Will not allow ecological data to be treated as background noise. Vireo's recurring signal: "The data was always there."
